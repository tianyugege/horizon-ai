## [MOTHRAG: Graph-Free Multi-Hop Retrieval Outperforms Graph-Based Systems](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1ukotww/p_mothretrieval_graphfree_multihop_retrieval_via/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

MOTHRAG is a newly open-sourced multi-hop retrieval framework that eliminates the need for knowledge graphs by using a graph-free dense index with query-time orchestration. It achieves higher accuracy than leading graph-based systems on benchmarks like HotpotQA while enabling efficient incremental updates without costly re-indexing. This development is significant because it addresses a major practical limitation of graph-based multi-hop retrieval systems: the expensive and time-consuming re-indexing required when data changes. MOTHRAG's approach reduces operational costs and improves flexibility, benefiting applications with frequently updated corpora such as news, support tickets, or financial data. MOTHRAG uses a graph-free dense index and orchestrates retrieval at query time without requiring GPUs, costing about $0.03 per query on commodity APIs. It outperforms GraphRAG, HippoRAG, and RAPTOR on HotpotQA, 2WikiMultiHopQA, and MuSiQue benchmarks in accuracy and F1 scores, though it still faces challenges on MuSiQue due to retrieval recall bottlenecks.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/Annual-Commercial563 · Jul 1, 15:26

**Background**: Multi-hop retrieval refers to the process of retrieving multiple pieces of evidence from a corpus to answer complex queries that require reasoning over several documents. Traditional systems often rely on knowledge graphs built offline to guide retrieval, but these graphs require costly re-indexing whenever the underlying data changes. Query-time orchestration is an approach where retrieval decisions are made dynamically during query execution, potentially improving efficiency and adaptability.
